Accountability and Design Choices

Source of Dataset

The data is collected by AWARE, which captures hardware and human-based data. The data was collected from my personal iphone from Feb 18th to Feb 25th. I used my second mobile phone for data collection instead of my primary one.

Accessibility of the Dataset

I have the full control over the study, and the data is about my personal mobile phone usage. Therefore, I should be the only one who is able to access the dataset. However, while AWARE claims that they "do not log personal information, such as phone numbers or contacts information", it is not clear if they can access the usage data of individuals.

Summary of the Dataset

The data is collected from my second phone from Feb 18th to Feb 25th, which is mainly used for entertainment, twitter, Instagrams, etc. (I use my primary phone only for work and study for the sake of self control). The analysis focuses on 3 sensors battery, screen, and wifi. Interesting variables include

Battery Level - Battery level in range 0 to 100
Wifi - There are 3 possibilities to 1) the phone connects to CMU-SECURE when at school or 2) connects to AntiNeuralNetwork (this is the name of my home wifi) or xfinitywifi when at home 3) None if no wifi is available
Screen Status - one of the following: 0=off, 1=on, 2=locked, 3=unlocked
Timestamp - the timestamp in format '%Y-%m-%d %H:%M:%S' for the collected data

Design Choices

For visualization, I first created statistical summary tables (mean, range, median) for the continuous variable (battery level), and frequency tables for categorical variables (screen status and wifi). Histograms for continuous varibales and pie charts for categorical variables are created to display the data distribution for each feature and detect skewness. I then created graphs that display relationship between variables. For example, zoomable timeline is used to show how battery level changes over time. For each hour, I inspected the screen status, wifi usage, and median battery level in attempt to detect any interesting patterns. The three graphs all have hours on the horizontal axis, and are placed vertically for easier comparison.
